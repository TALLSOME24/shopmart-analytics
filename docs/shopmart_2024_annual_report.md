## Executive Summary
ShopSmart generated total revenue of ₦14,221,350 in 2024 across 
50 transactions from 50 unique customers in Lagos, Abuja 
and Port Harcourt. Electronics dominated revenue at 81.4% 
of total sales. December was the strongest month while 
February recorded the lowest performance. Key concerns 
include zero repeat customers and heavy revenue 
concentration in one category.

---

## Revenue Analysis

**Total Revenue:** ₦14,221,350
**Total Transactions:** 50
**Average Order Value:** ₦284,427

**Monthly Performance:**
The business showed inconsistent monthly revenue throughout 
2024. The strongest month was December with ₦2,743,000 in revenue 
while the weakest was February with ₦434,250. A significant revenue 
spike in September was driven by iPhone 15 Pro purchases. 
The Q4 period (October-December) delivered the strongest 
sustained performance of the year.

**Running Total Milestone:**
ShopSmart crossed the ₦10M cumulative revenue mark in 
October 2024, finishing the year at ₦14,221,350.

## Key Insights

- Electronics accounts for 81.4% of revenue making 
ShopSmart heavily dependent on one category
- Zero repeat customers in 2024 indicates a complete 
absence of customer loyalty or retention strategy
- Lagos drives nearly 70% of total revenue — 
geographic concentration is a business risk
- February consistently underperforms — no promotional 
strategy exists for slow periods
- September revenue spike (+₦1.8M MoM) driven by a 
single product launch suggests over-reliance on 
individual hero products

---

## Recommendations for 2025

1. **Launch a Customer Loyalty Programme** — with 0% 
repeat purchase rate, converting even 20% of existing 
customers into repeat buyers could add millions in 
revenue at near-zero acquisition cost

2. **Diversify Category Revenue** — develop Home Goods 
and Fashion categories which show premium products 
but low volume. Target 70/30 Electronics vs other 
categories by end of 2025

3. **Expand Abuja and Port Harcourt Operations** — 
both cities are significantly underperforming Lagos. 
Targeted regional marketing and inventory investment 
could double orders in these markets

4. **Introduce February Promotions** — a targeted 
discount campaign in January-February could reduce 
the seasonal revenue dip significantly

5. **Reduce Hero Product Dependency** — with iPhone 15 Pro 
alone accounting for 15.5% of total revenue, supply 
chain or pricing risks on one product could materially 
impact the business
